1. Difference between div and span


Div: is a block level elemnt, it is bound to take the whole width of the screen. It is used for grouping to elements in HTML documents, means div should be used wrap section of document.
span: is a inline element, means it only takes the space that is required for it. span should be used to wrap small portion of text and imges, etc.

5. Difference between block and inline


Block: the elements always begin with a new line and it extends from left to right. It takes the full width available on the screen. Browser automatically adds some space(margin) before and after the element. Ex:- div, h1 to h6, p, ol, ul, table, etc.
Inline: does not begin on a new line. It only takes space that is necessary for it. Ex:- span, img, input, a, label.

6. Difference between em and strong


Strong: Strong tag should be used to indicate strong imposrtance or urgency of text. Em: Em tag is used to represent stress emphasis, like when you read a emphasized text in a different tone of voice. Both can be used together where it makes sense.
